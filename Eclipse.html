THE SOLAR ECLIPSE!

Click the picture above to learn when, where, and how to see solar eclipses.

A solar eclipse is when the moon passes between the sun and the earth, and partially or fully blocks the sun.

The solar eclipse this year will occur on August 21, 2017. You need special glasses that reflect the light away from your eyes, yet you can still view the sun. Only during a total solar eclipse, called totality, is when you can view the sun's corona with the naked eye. The sun's corona is the outlining of the sun when the moon coveres it.. That is the point when you do not need to wear any gear, like the kind below.

Click the picture below to get directed to a website on Solar Eclipse glasses safety.

There are other ways of viewing solar eclipses than watching the sun directly. You can view a solar eclipse online; live streams are posted by websites that record the solar eclipses with certain filters to keep the camera safe. To view the upcoming solar eclipse on August 21, 2017 live, click www.exploratorium.edu. Another way to view a partial solar eclipse without looking at is Pinhole Projection. This is where you print an image of the sun, and make a small hole, out of an index card for example, and direct the index card to the picture, and toward the sun. This will show the sun's state without even looking at it!
